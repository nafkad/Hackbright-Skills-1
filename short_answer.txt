8) What is the difference between `git add` and `git commit`?

Your answer: "git add" will track the file(s) of your choice; it adds a new or changed file in the working directory to the Git staging area. 
Whereas, "git commit -a -m" will add the file(s) of your choice with a comment; it captures a snapshot of the new or changed files in the woring directory to the local repository.
